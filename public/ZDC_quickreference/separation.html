Wake Turbulence Separation Minima

  • Medium then Light: 2 minutes
  • Heavy then Medium: 2 minutes
  • Heavy then Light: 3 minutes
  • Super then Medium: 3 minutes
  • Super then Light: 4 minutes

Same Runway Separation Minima

... to be used when successive aircraft are NOT on the same route, Wake Turbulence separation sitations do not apply, and aircraft can be given headings diverging by 15 degrees or more.
  • Single- or Multi-Engine Prop then Single-Engine Prop: 3000ft / 0.5nm
  • Single- or Multi-Engine Prop then Multi-Engine Prop: 4500ft / 0.75nm
  • Any Other Combination: 6000ft / 1nm

Radar Separation Minima

... to be used when successive aircraft are NOT on the same route, Wake Turbulence separation sitations do not apply, but aircraft cannot be given headings diverging by 15 degrees or more -- standard radar separation in a terminal area is 3 nautical miles (approximately 1 minute).

Same Route Separation Minima

... unless otherwise coordinated with Departure, commonly accepted / preferred separation between two aircraft on the same departure route is 3 minutes / 10 nautical miles. For events with traffic metering, it may be increased for certain destinations without limit.

Mixing Departures With Arrivals: Recommendations

  • IN POSITION: expect 45 sec for takeoff roll; give takeoff clearance with arriving aircraft no closer than 2-3nm
  • HOLDING SHORT: expect 90 sec for takeoff roll; give takeoff clearance with arriving aircraft no closer than 4-6nm
